Red Rock Adventures

The Plan

Picture this: it's early 2020, there are only slight hints of the chaos to come. No one has predicted the end of the world as we know it, so what better time for a roadtrip! As avid explorers, my boyfriend and I planned my first ever visit to the magical red rocks of eastern Utah. While taking the scenic route we made a few very important stops along the way (Did that sign just say alpaca farm!? Pull over!). As March approched, right at the cusp of disaster, we we loaded up the car with camping gear and more snacks than anyone should ever consume, then began our adventure. Final destination: Moab, Utah.

First Stop: Leadville, CO

After enduring the desolate, windmill studded landscape of Kansas and a brief overnight stay in Denver we veered slightly off course to visit the quant town of Leadville, CO. Nestled in the mountains, the town is well known for it's high elevation (10,200 feet to be exact) and mountain biking trails,but we made pit stop for a little store called Melanzana. Their handmade outdoor clothing is the best of it's kind AND you can't get in anywhere else. When we arrived we found the streets were transformed into snow covered slopes and horsback riders almost outnumbered the tourists. We'd stumbled into their skijorning festival a sport during which a skiier is pulled by horse along the track, over ramps and other obstacles. After watching the festivites and grabbing a few amazingly cozy Mellys we continued our journey.

Second Stop: Moab, UT

This small town, surrounded by national park land, was our base of operations for the next three days and man, was it perfect. Local businesses line the (only slightly gaudy) desert themed streets, all of which were easily within walking distance of our hotel. It was the perfect spot to return to after a long day of hiking and exploration.

Arches, National Park

At the break of dawn, the best place to watch the sunrise over red rock formations, is by far at Arches. We hiked up to the Delicate Arch, the park’s most iconic rock formation, at day break. With a spectacular view of the entire park from above, the trail was well worth the elevation gain. After that initial trail, we drove around the park and completed a few shorter trails. An entire day is easily spent within this massive park.

Hauer Ranch

I can't think of a better way to explore western landscape than on horseback. We went on a ride into remote BLM land to see breathtaking views of the landscape. The tour took us through several set locations for famous western films, the most notable being John Wayne film “Rio Grande.”

Off the Beaten Path

Beginning in Moab, the Upper Colorado River Scenic Byway (Route 128) follows the Colorado River through a breathtaking valley. Along this route, we found a short hike through the canyon that led to a small natural bridge. The route also meandered through vast ranchland with the La Sal mountain range looming in the background.

Homeward Bound

After 3 days in Moab, it was time to begin the treck home. Needless to say, we took the long way. We decided to make a pit stop in Durango, CO as well as a quick visit to the Great Sand Dunes National Park. Both stops were well worth the hours added to our drive. The dunes were massive and beautiful. If we'd had more time, we would've grabbed a sled and (which the park provided) and flown down the dunes. But, that will have to wait until next time!

About Me
